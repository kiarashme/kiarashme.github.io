KeepAway

Distracting your dog from destroying your house

My role: conducting preliminary research on various dog toy solutions, designing and implementing prototypes with Arduino and an Estimote Beacon, testing the prototype with my boyfriend's puppy.

I worked on this application with Purcell Liddy and Laura Ascher.

Problem

Dogs can get into all sorts of messes (eating shoes, destroying pillows etc) while their owner isn’t watching.

Solution

KeepAway is an interactive dog toy that is designed to move and light up if the dog approaches a certain spot in the house.

Prototyping: First round

The collar component

We started by prototyping the component that goes on the dog’s collar.

The blue container is attached to a collar, and holds the proximity sensor. The container can be securely fastened to any dog’s collar.

This prototype shows a rectangular container to hold the sensor, however the final design will feature rounded edges to ensure comfortable wearing experience for the dog.

Prototyping: Second Round

The toy component

The next step was activating the lights on the toy remotely. I used arduino, LED lights, a bluetooth module, and an Android app that could activate the arduino via Bluetooth.

Laura made a hedgehog-like cover for the toy out of felt. We put the LEDs through the spikes so that the lights would be obviously exposed.

Prototyping: Third Round

For the final round of prototyping, we built a wire structure and a base on wheels that would hold the toy. We took the Android app that activated Arduino via Bluetooth for our first demo and used it as a sample to write our own app that would also respond to beacon activation.

In the end we had an app which allowed us to activate the toy manually and as a response to the activation of the proximity sensor.

Takeaways and future work

More than anything, this was a really fun learning experience that exposed me to solving problems that I've never encountered before. With no prior experience of working with Arduino or beacons I found the necessary resources to put together a viable solution. As a product, this still needs a lot of work, such as making the toy durable against the dog's rough play, as well as specifying the distance at which the proximity sensor is activated.
